# Engineering Management Playbook  
### Jesse Lassiter

Engineering leadership guidance shaped by experience in enterprise IT operations, platform reliability, and cross-functional product collaboration. 

This playbook outlines leadership practices for managing engineering and technical teams within enterprise environments.

It reflects principles developed across infrastructure, product, and platform leadership roles.

---

## 1. Define Clear Ownership

Every system, service, and initiative should have defined accountability. Clear ownership reduces ambiguity and strengthens execution.

---

## 2. Promote Structured Delivery

Use disciplined roadmapping, backlog prioritization, and milestone tracking to ensure predictable delivery.

---

## 3. Balance Stability and Innovation

Protect core systems while enabling strategic advancement. Engineering maturity requires balancing reliability with forward progress.

---

## 4. Strengthen Observability

Encourage teams to build systems that are measurable, traceable, and diagnosable.

---

## 5. Develop Future Leaders

Engineering management includes mentorship, skill development, and succession planning.

---

## 6. Improve Through Retrospective Analysis

Post-incident reviews and operational retrospectives enable structured learning and improvement.

---

## 7. Foster Cross-Functional Collaboration

Strong engineering outcomes require alignment with product, operations, and executive stakeholders.
